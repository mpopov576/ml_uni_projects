**This project is based on a dataset containing information about cities around the world and various factors related to quality of life.**

Quality of life is a complex concept influenced by economic, environmental, and social indicators. However, cities in the dataset are not explicitly ranked, making it difficult to directly compare them.

The goal of this project is to analyze the relationships between factors such as income, cost of living, air quality, public transport, and happiness, and to apply unsupervised machine learning techniques to identify natural groupings of cities.

Based on these insights, the project develops a custom index that estimates quality of life (where lower values indicate better conditions) and builds a model capable of labeling both existing and new cities.

By uncovering hidden patterns in the data, this project aims to provide meaningful insights into what defines a high quality of life across different urban environments.
